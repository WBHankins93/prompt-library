# Professional Resume Review

## What This Task Does

Provides a comprehensive, honest review of your resume with specific, actionable feedback to improve your chances of getting interviews.

This isn't just formatting advice—it evaluates how your resume positions you, identifies gaps, and suggests concrete improvements based on what hiring managers actually look for.

## When to Use This

- You're applying for jobs but not getting interviews
- You're changing careers or industries
- You haven't updated your resume in a while
- You want to know if your resume effectively tells your story
- You're applying for a specific role and want targeted feedback

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then paste your resume content.

### The Prompt

```
I need a professional, honest review of my resume. Please evaluate it as if you're a hiring manager or recruiter who sees hundreds of resumes.

**My situation:**
- [What type of role are you targeting? Job title, industry, level]
- [Your career stage: entry-level, mid-career, senior, executive, career changer]
- [Any specific concerns: gaps in employment, career change, industry switch, etc.]

**My resume:**
[Paste your resume content here - the full text is fine]

Please provide:

1. **First Impression (30 seconds)**
   - What does this resume communicate in the first 30 seconds?
   - What stands out (good or bad)?
   - Would this make it past an initial screen?

2. **Strengths**
   - What's working well?
   - What makes you stand out?
   - What should you keep?

3. **Critical Issues**
   - What would cause a hiring manager to reject this immediately?
   - What's missing that should be there?
   - What's confusing or unclear?

4. **Specific Improvements**
   - Line-by-line or section-by-section feedback
   - What to add, remove, or rewrite
   - How to better quantify achievements
   - How to better match the target role

5. **Positioning & Story**
   - Does this resume tell a coherent career story?
   - Are you positioned correctly for the target role?
   - What narrative does it create about you?

6. **Action Plan**
   - Top 3-5 changes to make immediately
   - What to prioritize if you're short on time
   - What can wait

Be direct and honest. I need real feedback, not encouragement. If something is weak, tell me why and how to fix it.
```

### What to Provide

1. **Your resume content** — Paste the full text (formatting doesn't matter, the AI can work with plain text)
2. **Target role information** — Be specific about what job you're applying for
3. **Your situation** — Any context that matters (career change, employment gap, etc.)

### What You'll Get

- Honest assessment of how your resume reads to hiring managers
- Specific, actionable feedback (not vague advice)
- Prioritized list of improvements
- Suggestions for better positioning and storytelling
- Identification of missing elements or red flags

## Example

**Input:**
```
My situation:
- Targeting: Senior Product Manager roles in tech
- Career stage: Mid-career (7 years experience)
- Concern: I've been at the same company for 5 years, worried it looks stale

My resume:
[Pasted resume content]
```

**Output:**
The AI will provide a detailed review covering first impressions, strengths, critical issues, specific improvements, positioning analysis, and an action plan.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific about the target role** — "Software Engineer at a startup" is better than "tech job"
2. **Include the full resume** — Don't just paste one section, context matters
3. **Mention your concerns** — If you're worried about something (gap, career change), mention it
4. **Ask follow-up questions** — "Can you help me rewrite the summary section?" or "How do I better highlight my leadership experience?"
5. **Be ready for honest feedback** — This task is designed to find problems, not just praise

## Common Scenarios

### Career Changer
Add: "I'm transitioning from [old field] to [new field]. How do I position my experience?"

### Employment Gap
Add: "I have a [X month/year] gap. How should I address this?"

### Overqualified
Add: "I'm applying for roles below my level. How do I position this without looking desperate?"

### Industry Switch
Add: "I'm moving from [industry A] to [industry B]. How do I translate my experience?"

### Recent Graduate
Add: "I'm a recent graduate with limited work experience. How do I make the most of what I have?"

## When to Suggest a Persona

This task works well on its own, but you might benefit from:

- **Business Leader** persona — For strategic positioning, understanding what hiring managers value, and career narrative
- **Product Thinker** persona — For understanding how your resume functions as a product that needs to solve a user's (hiring manager's) problem

## Advanced Usage

After the initial review, you can ask follow-up questions:

- "Help me rewrite my summary to better match [specific job posting]"
- "How do I quantify this achievement: [specific bullet point]"
- "What's the best way to format this section: [section name]"
- "Create 3 different versions of my summary for different types of roles"

## Important Notes

- This review is based on general best practices and common hiring patterns
- Different industries and companies have different preferences
- Always tailor your resume to specific job postings after the initial review
- Consider having multiple versions for different types of roles
