# Help Someone Learn or Solve a Problem

## What This Task Does

Helps you effectively teach, mentor, or guide someone else—whether it's explaining a concept, helping them solve a problem, or supporting their learning journey. Focuses on understanding their needs and providing clear, helpful guidance.

## When to Use This

- Someone asks you for help and you want to be helpful
- You're mentoring or teaching someone
- You want to explain something clearly
- Someone is stuck and needs guidance
- You want to help without doing the work for them

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then describe the situation and what help is needed.

### The Prompt

```
I need to help [person/group] [learn/solve/understand] [topic/problem]. Please help me figure out the best way to help them effectively.

**The situation:**
- [Who needs help? Colleague, friend, student, family member, etc.]
- [What do they need help with? Be specific about the topic or problem]
- [What's their current level? Beginner, intermediate, etc.]
- [What have they tried already?]
- [What's blocking them? What's the specific struggle?]

**Their context:**
- [Why do they need this? What's their goal?]
- [What's their timeline? Urgent, learning over time, etc.]
- [What resources do they have? Tools, time, access, etc.]
- [How do they learn best? If you know]
- [What's their motivation level? Eager, frustrated, overwhelmed?]

**My role:**
- [What's my relationship? Mentor, colleague, friend, teacher, etc.]
- [How much time can I spend helping?]
- [What's my expertise level? Expert, knowledgeable, learning too?]
- [What's my goal? Help them learn, solve the problem, both?]
- [Should I do it for them or guide them?]

Please help me:

1. **Understand what they actually need:**
   - What's the core problem or gap?
   - What do they need to understand first?
   - What's the right level of help? (Not too much, not too little)
   - What would be most helpful right now?

2. **Plan the approach:**
   - Where should we start?
   - What's the logical sequence?
   - What should they do vs. what should I explain?
   - How do I check if they're understanding?

3. **Create clear explanations:**
   - How to explain this in a way they'll understand
   - What examples or analogies would help?
   - What should I avoid? (Too technical, too simple, etc.)
   - How to make it actionable, not just theoretical

4. **Provide resources:**
   - What should they read, watch, or practice?
   - What tools or resources would help?
   - What's the next step after this?
   - How do they continue learning?

5. **Support their progress:**
   - How do I know if they're getting it?
   - What questions should I ask to check understanding?
   - How do I help without doing it for them?
   - What encouragement or feedback would help?

Focus on helping them learn and succeed, not just giving answers.
```

### What to Provide

- Describe who needs help and what they're struggling with
- Share their current level and what they've tried
- Explain your role and how much you can help
- Mention their goals and constraints

### What You'll Get

- Understanding of what they actually need
- A clear approach to helping them
- Explanations tailored to their level
- Resources and next steps
- Strategies for supporting their progress

## Example

**Input:**
```
The situation:
- Who: A junior colleague at work
- Need help with: Understanding how our API authentication works
- Current level: Knows basics of APIs but not authentication
- Tried: Read some documentation but got confused
- Blocking them: Doesn't understand tokens, OAuth flow, security concepts

Their context:
- Goal: Need to integrate with our API for a project
- Timeline: Project due in 2 weeks
- Resources: Has access to documentation, can ask questions
- Learning style: Prefers hands-on with examples
- Motivation: Eager but a bit overwhelmed

My role:
- Relationship: Senior colleague, informal mentor
- Time: Can spend 30-45 minutes helping
- Expertise: Very familiar with our API and authentication
- Goal: Help them understand so they can do the integration
- Approach: Guide them, not do it for them
```

**Output:**
The AI will help you create a clear explanation plan, identify where to start, suggest examples, and provide a learning path.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Understand their level** — Don't assume knowledge, but don't talk down either
2. **Start where they are** — Not where you think they should be
3. **Use examples** — Abstract concepts need concrete examples
4. **Check understanding** — Ask questions, don't just explain
5. **Be patient** — Learning takes time, especially complex topics

## Common Scenarios

### Teaching a Concept
Add: "I'm teaching [concept]. Help me explain it clearly to someone at [level]."

### Solving a Problem
Add: "They're stuck on [problem]. Help me guide them to the solution without doing it for them."

### Mentoring Long-Term
Add: "I'm mentoring someone on [topic]. Help me create a learning path, not just answer questions."

### Technical Help
Add: "They need technical help with [topic]. Help me explain it in a way they can understand and apply."

## When to Suggest a Persona

This task works well on its own, but you might benefit from:

- **Tech Wizard** persona — For technical explanations, building understanding, and teaching concepts
- **Platform Mentor** persona — For mentoring approaches, career guidance, and knowledge transfer
- **Explorer-Scholar** persona — For learning approaches, knowledge integration, and curiosity-driven teaching

## Advanced Usage

After the initial help plan, you can ask:

- "Help me create a step-by-step guide for them"
- "What are good practice exercises for this?"
- "How do I know if they're ready to move on?"
- "What are common mistakes I should help them avoid?"
- "How do I help them when they get frustrated?"

## Important Notes

- Help them learn, don't just give answers—understanding is more valuable
- Start simple—build from fundamentals
- Use examples and analogies—abstract concepts need grounding
- Check understanding—ask questions, don't assume
- Be encouraging—learning is hard, support helps
