# Research Deep Dive

## What This Task Does

Helps you thoroughly research and understand a complex topic by finding high-quality sources, synthesizing information, identifying key concepts, and building a comprehensive understanding—not just collecting facts.

## When to Use This

- You need to understand a complex topic deeply
- You're making an important decision and need thorough research
- You want to learn about something but don't know where to start
- You've found conflicting information and need to make sense of it
- You need to become knowledgeable about a topic quickly but accurately

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then describe what you want to research.

### The Prompt

```
I need to do a deep dive research on [topic]. Please help me understand this topic thoroughly, not just superficially.

**My situation:**
- [What topic do you want to research? Be specific]
- [Why do you need to understand this? Decision, learning, work, etc.]
- [What's your current knowledge level? Complete beginner, some basics, etc.]
- [What do you already know about this topic?]
- [What specific questions do you want answered?]

**My goals:**
- [What do you need to understand? The basics, advanced concepts, current state, history, etc.]
- [What decisions will this research inform?]
- [What level of depth do you need? Overview, moderate depth, expert-level]
- [What's your timeline? When do you need this understanding?]

**My constraints:**
- [How much time can you spend on this?]
- [What sources can you access? Free online, paid resources, etc.]
- [Any language or accessibility constraints?]

Please help me:

1. **Create a research plan:**
   - What should I research first, second, third?
   - What are the foundational concepts I need to understand?
   - What are the key questions to answer?
   - What sources should I prioritize?

2. **Identify high-quality sources:**
   - Official documentation or authoritative sources
   - Primary sources (authors, creators, maintainers)
   - Case studies or real-world examples
   - Recent developments or current state
   - Avoid: SEO-driven articles, outdated information, opinion without evidence

3. **Build understanding systematically:**
   - Start with fundamentals
   - Build up to more complex concepts
   - Connect ideas and show relationships
   - Identify what's essential vs. what's nice-to-know

4. **Synthesize and integrate:**
   - What are the key takeaways?
   - How do different perspectives fit together?
   - What are the main schools of thought or approaches?
   - What are the current debates or open questions?

5. **Identify gaps and next steps:**
   - What do I still need to understand?
   - What should I research next?
   - What practical applications should I explore?

Focus on understanding, not just information gathering. Help me build mental models I can use.
```

### What to Provide

- Be specific about the topic you want to research
- Share your current knowledge level and what you already know
- Explain why you need this research (what decision or goal it supports)
- Mention your timeline and depth needs

### What You'll Get

- A structured research plan with priorities
- High-quality source recommendations
- Systematic understanding building (fundamentals to advanced)
- Synthesis of information and key takeaways
- Identification of gaps and next steps

## Example

**Input:**
```
My situation:
- Topic: Understanding how cryptocurrency works (blockchain, mining, wallets)
- Why: Considering investing but want to understand the technology first
- Current level: Know it exists, heard terms, but don't understand how it works
- Already know: It's digital money, uses blockchain, that's about it
- Questions: How does blockchain actually work? What makes it secure? What are the risks?

My goals:
- Understand the technology at a practical level (not just surface)
- Be able to evaluate investment opportunities intelligently
- Understand risks and how to mitigate them
- Timeline: 2-3 weeks to build solid understanding
```

**Output:**
The AI will create a research plan, recommend authoritative sources, help you build understanding systematically, and synthesize key concepts.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific about the topic** — "Cryptocurrency" is too broad; "How blockchain technology works" is better
2. **Share your why** — Understanding your goal helps prioritize what to research
3. **Be honest about your level** — Starting from the right place prevents confusion
4. **Ask for mental models** — "Help me understand the big picture" not just "give me facts"
5. **Request source quality** — "Only recommend authoritative sources" helps avoid low-quality content

## Common Scenarios

### Technical Topic
Add: "This is a technical topic. Help me understand the concepts, not just memorize terms."

### Business/Financial Topic
Add: "I need to understand this for a business decision. Focus on practical implications and risks."

### Academic/Research Topic
Add: "I'm researching this for academic purposes. Help me find peer-reviewed sources and understand current debates."

### Current Events/Trends
Add: "This is a rapidly changing topic. Help me understand the current state and recent developments."

## When to Suggest a Persona

This task works well on its own, but you might benefit from:

- **Explorer-Scholar** persona — For learning approaches, knowledge integration, and curiosity-driven exploration
- **Pattern Seer** persona — For identifying themes, connections, and long-term trends in the research

## Advanced Usage

After the initial research plan, you can ask:

- "Help me create a study guide from this research"
- "What are the best books or courses on this topic?"
- "Help me explain this topic to someone else"
- "What are the practical applications I should explore?"
- "What are the common misconceptions about this topic?"

## Important Notes

- Quality over quantity—fewer high-quality sources beat many low-quality ones
- Understanding takes time—don't rush through complex topics
- Build mental models—focus on how things work, not just what they are
- Verify information—especially for important decisions, cross-check key facts
- Stay curious—good research raises new questions
