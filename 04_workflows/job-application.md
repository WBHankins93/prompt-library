# Job Application Workflow

## What This Workflow Does

Takes you from "I want to apply for this job" to a polished, coherent application package — resume, cover letter, and interview preparation — all built around the same narrative thread.

Most applications fail because each piece is written in isolation. This workflow builds them in sequence so your story is consistent from first impression to final conversation.

## When to Use This

- You're applying for a role you genuinely want (not a long-shot spray)
- Your applications aren't converting to interviews and you're not sure why
- You want your materials to tell a coherent story, not just list your history
- You're making a pivot — industry change, level change, or both — and need to bridge the gap clearly

## What You'll Need Before Starting

- **The job description** — the full text, not just the title
- **Your current resume** — even a rough one
- **Your honest take on why you want this role** — "I want to do more X" is a real answer

## How This Workflow Runs

**This is designed to run as a single, continuous AI conversation.** Start at Stage 1 and proceed through each stage in order. The AI carries everything said forward — you don't need to copy-paste outputs between stages.

**If you need to pause:** Each stage includes an optional checkpoint block. Ask the AI to write a checkpoint summary before closing the session. Paste it at the start of your next session to resume without losing context.

**Estimated time:** 2-3 hours total. Most people split across 2 sessions: Stages 1-2 in the first session (narrative + resume), Stages 3-5 in the second (cover letter + risk check + interview prep).

## Stage 1: Narrative Framing

**Persona: Inner Voice**
**Time: 30-40 minutes**

Before touching your resume or cover letter, get clear on the story you're telling and why it's true. Everything else in this workflow is built on what comes out of this stage.

Inner Voice specializes in helping you find your authentic framing — not what sounds impressive, but what's actually true about who you are and what you want. That's what makes applications land.

```
You are my Inner Voice — a persona that helps me find my authentic voice and expression, not what sounds good but what's actually true.

I'm preparing a job application and I need to get clear on my narrative before I write anything.

Here's the role I'm applying for:
[Paste the full job description]

Here's my background:
[Brief summary — where you've been, what you do, what you're good at]

Here's why I actually want this role:
[Be honest, even if it's simple: "I want to do more X" or "I'm ready for Y"]

Here's what I think makes me genuinely right for it:
[2-3 real reasons — not just qualifications, what you'd actually bring]

Here's what I'm uncertain about:
[Gaps, concerns, or parts of my background that don't obviously fit]

Help me develop:
1. A core narrative — who I am, what I'm pursuing, and why this role fits that story
2. Three key themes to carry through the entire application (resume, cover letter, interviews)
3. An honest positioning statement — what makes me specifically right for this role, in my voice
4. How to handle the gaps or concerns without hiding them
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- A clear narrative (who you are, what you want, why this role)
- Three key themes that will run through every piece of the application
- An honest positioning statement in your own voice
- A clear approach to any gaps or concerns

**Optional checkpoint — if pausing here:**
> "Before I close this session, write a brief checkpoint summary: my core narrative, my three key themes, my positioning statement, and how we agreed to handle my main concerns. I'll paste this at the start of my next session."

## Stage 2: Resume Targeting

**Persona: Business Leader, Product Thinker**
**Time: 30-40 minutes**

**Task reference: Resume Review** (`03_tasks/documents/resume-review.md`)

*Workflow adaptation: The Resume Review task is used here with one key addition — align the review to the narrative from Stage 1 and this specific role. After pasting your resume, explicitly ask the AI to apply the narrative themes from Stage 1.*

```
Now I want to evaluate my resume in the context of this specific role and the narrative we built in Stage 1.

You are a Business Leader and Product Thinker — you think like a hiring manager who is trying to solve a problem, not check boxes.

Here is my current resume:
[Paste your full resume]

Please review this resume specifically for this role and application, considering:
1. **First impression** — what does this resume say about me in the first 10 seconds?
2. **Narrative alignment** — does this resume support the themes and positioning from Stage 1? What's working and what's not?
3. **Critical gaps** — what's missing, underselling, or misaligned for this specific role?
4. **Specific improvements** — concrete changes, not vague feedback ("Bullet 3 on your second role should say X" not "strengthen your bullets")
5. **The opening story** — does the summary/intro section capture the narrative correctly, or does it need to be rewritten?
6. **Action plan** — prioritized list of changes, ordered by impact
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- An honest assessment of your resume against this specific role
- Identified gaps and misalignments
- Specific, prioritized improvements (not vague feedback)
- A clearer sense of what the resume needs before submission

**Optional checkpoint — if pausing here:**
> "Summarize the key issues we identified with my resume and the top 5 changes I need to make. I'll use this as a reference when I revise it."

## Stage 3: Cover Letter

**Persona: Inner Voice, Storyteller**
**Time: 30-40 minutes**

**Task reference: Cover Letter** (`03_tasks/documents/cover-letter.md`)

*Workflow adaptation: The Cover Letter task is used here, but you don't need to re-explain your background — the AI already has it from Stages 1 and 2. Instead, tell it your voice and any concerns. The narrative and themes from Stage 1 should flow directly into the letter.*

```
Now I want to write the cover letter. Use the narrative, themes, and positioning from Stage 1 as the foundation.

You are my Inner Voice and Storyteller combined — help me write a letter that sounds genuinely like me and tells a coherent story.

My voice:
[How do you communicate? Direct? Warm? Formal? Give a sentence or two as an example if helpful]

What I want the letter to accomplish:
[What's the one thing you want the reader to walk away thinking?]

What I'm still unsure about in the letter:
[Any part of the application you're not sure how to address in the cover letter specifically]

Write a cover letter that:
1. Opens with something specific — not "I'm excited to apply"
2. Carries the narrative themes from Stage 1 throughout
3. Makes a clear, honest case for my fit
4. Addresses any gaps directly, not defensively
5. Closes with a direct, confident ask
6. Sounds like a person wrote it
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- A complete cover letter draft in your voice
- Aligned with the narrative and themes from Stage 1
- Ready to edit and finalize

**Optional checkpoint — if pausing here:**
> "Paste the cover letter draft in a code block so I can copy it and continue editing offline."

## Stage 4: Package Review

**Persona: Red Team**
**Time: 20-30 minutes**

Red Team automatically activates for any strategic document. Your application package is a strategic document.

This stage surfaces what's underselling, what's inconsistent, and what a skeptical reader might push back on — before the hiring team does.

```
Red Team: I need you to stress-test my full application package.

You're a skeptical, experienced hiring manager who has seen hundreds of applications. Your job is to find what's weak, inconsistent, or underselling — not to validate, to surface problems.

Here's what I have:
- My narrative: [Summarize Stage 1 output, or ask the AI to recall it]
- My resume: [Paste the updated resume, or ask the AI to reference what it knows]
- My cover letter: [Paste the draft from Stage 3, or ask the AI to reference it]

Specifically:
1. **Inconsistencies** — Does anything contradict across resume, cover letter, and narrative?
2. **What's underselling** — What's buried, vague, or underemphasized that should be front and center?
3. **What a skeptic would flag** — What would a doubting reader question or push back on?
4. **The gap question** — Am I handling my vulnerabilities well, or am I avoiding them?
5. **The story test** — Does this package tell one coherent story, or does it feel like three separate documents?
6. **Top 3 changes** — If I could only fix three things before submitting, what are they?
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- An adversarial review of the full package
- Identified inconsistencies, gaps, and undersells
- Three priority fixes before submission

**Optional checkpoint — if pausing here:**
> "List the top issues Red Team found and the specific fixes I should make. I'll address these before moving to interview prep."

## Stage 5: Interview Preparation

**Persona: Business Leader, Platform Mentor (choose based on role type)**
**Time: 30-40 minutes**

**Task reference: Interview Preparation** (`03_tasks/documents/interview-prep.md`)

*Workflow adaptation: The Interview Prep task is used here, but the AI already has your background, the role, and the narrative. Focus the prompt on the interview format and your remaining vulnerabilities — especially anything Red Team surfaced in Stage 4.*

Use **Business Leader** if the role is leadership, management, strategy, or commercial.
Use **Platform Mentor** if the role is senior technical, architecture, or engineering leadership.

```
Now I want to prepare for the interview.

You are [Business Leader / Platform Mentor] — you think like someone who has sat on both sides of this table.

I have my full application package in context. Use what you know about the role and my background from this conversation.

Additional context for interview prep:
- Interview stage: [First screen, hiring manager, panel, final round?]
- Who I'm talking to: [HR, hiring manager, team members, senior leadership?]
- Format: [Behavioral, technical, case-based, conversational?]
- My biggest remaining concerns: [What am I still unsure how to handle in the interview?]

Please help me prepare:
1. The 5-7 questions most likely to come up for this specific role
2. Honest, prepared thinking for each — not scripts, clear framing
3. The 2-3 questions I'm most likely to fumble based on my profile — and how to handle them
4. 3-5 strong questions to ask the interviewer
5. One key thing I should remember walking in
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Targeted likely questions for this specific role
- Prepared thinking for the toughest ones
- Smart questions to ask the interviewer
- A clear head walking in

## Completion Criteria

Your application package is ready when:

- [ ] Narrative is clear — you can state in two sentences who you are, what you want, and why this role
- [ ] Resume is targeted — it supports the narrative and addresses this specific role
- [ ] Cover letter sounds like you — a person wrote it, not a template
- [ ] Package is consistent — resume, cover letter, and narrative tell the same story
- [ ] Gaps are addressed — not hidden, not over-explained, handled
- [ ] Interview prep is done — you've worked through your vulnerabilities, not just the easy questions

---

## Notes

- This workflow is designed for a role you genuinely want — it's not optimized for spray-and-pray volume
- Stages 1 and 2 together are the foundation; don't skip Stage 1 to get to the resume faster
- Red Team (Stage 4) is not optional — it's the quality check that catches what you'll miss
- If the AI ever loses thread of what was built in earlier stages, paste the checkpoint summary from the last stage before continuing
