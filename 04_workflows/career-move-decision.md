# Career Move Decision Framework

## What This Workflow Does

Takes you from "I'm thinking about making a career move" to a clear, confident decision — with the financial picture modeled, the strategic logic tested, and the emotional reality acknowledged.

Most career decisions fail because they're made on one dimension (the money, the title, the escape from a bad situation) while ignoring the others. This workflow forces an integrated view: strategic fit, financial reality, identity alignment, and risk — before you say yes or no.

## When to Use This

- You have an offer on the table and need to decide
- You're actively exploring a move and need to clarify what you're actually optimizing for
- You're considering a career pivot — industry, function, or level — and want to think it through rigorously
- You're choosing between two offers or two paths and the right answer isn't obvious
- You're considering leaving something without a clear destination and need help thinking it through

## What You'll Need Before Starting

- **Current situation** — role, compensation (total), trajectory, what's working and what isn't
- **The move you're considering** — the specific opportunity, offer details, or pivot you're evaluating
- **Your honest read on why** — what's driving this? Be specific and honest.

> **New here?** See the [Getting Started guide](getting-started.md) for how to load personas and run each stage.

## How This Workflow Runs

**Run as a single conversation.** The AI builds an integrated picture of your situation across all stages. Jumping to the decision in Stage 4 without doing Stage 1 produces worse decisions.

If you're choosing between two specific offers, run Stage 2 twice — once for each.

**Estimated time:** 2-3 hours. Stages 1-2 can be one session; Stages 3-4 a second.

## Stage 1: Strategic Clarity

**Persona: Career Strategist**
**Time: 35-45 minutes**

Before you evaluate any specific move, get clear on what you're actually optimizing for. The Career Strategist's job is to turn "I want something better" into a specific, answerable question.

```
You are my Career Strategist — a persona that helps me make deliberate, high-leverage career decisions and build career capital over time.

I'm considering a career move and need to get clear on the strategic picture before I evaluate the specific opportunity.

My current situation:
[Role, company size and type, tenure, compensation (approximate), your sense of your trajectory]

What I'm considering moving to:
[The specific opportunity, pivot, or general direction — as specific as you can be]

Why I'm considering it (be honest):
[What's actually driving this? Escape from something? Pulled toward something? Money? Title? A specific type of work? All of the above?]

What I believe matters most to me right now in a career:
[Compensation, growth, autonomy, impact, learning, stability, flexibility — rank what's actually true, not what sounds good]

Help me:
1. Clarify what I'm actually optimizing for — what does "better" mean for me specifically right now?
2. Assess my current position — am I building career capital or depleting it? What's my trajectory?
3. Evaluate whether this move direction builds or reduces optionality — does it open more doors or narrow them?
4. Identify the question I'm not asking — what am I not looking at that I should be?
5. Define the criteria I should use to evaluate the specific opportunity
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Clear articulation of what you're actually optimizing for
- An honest read on your current trajectory
- Evaluation criteria for the specific opportunity
- The question you should be asking that you weren't

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Summarize what I'm optimizing for, my assessment of my current trajectory, and the criteria I should use to evaluate this move. I'll use this in the next stages."

## Stage 2: Financial and Compensation Reality

**Persona: Finance Dragon, Career Strategist**
**Time: 30-40 minutes**

Total compensation analysis. This stage models the full financial picture of the move — including what's often hidden: equity, benefits, tax implications, and the compounding cost of getting the number wrong.

```
You are my Finance Dragon and Career Strategist together — help me understand the full financial picture of this career move.

My current compensation:
[Base salary, bonus structure and realistic payout, equity (vesting schedule, current value), benefits (healthcare, retirement match, etc.), any other perks with real value]

The new opportunity's compensation:
[Same breakdown for the new role — base, bonus, equity, benefits, start date and any signing bonus]

My location / tax situation:
[If there's a location change involved, note it — state tax differences matter]

Questions to address:
1. What's my true current total compensation when everything is counted correctly?
2. What's the new opportunity's true total compensation — and where is the offer stronger vs. weaker than it appears?
3. What's the real first-year financial picture — factoring in unvested equity I'd be leaving, signing bonus structure, and any gaps?
4. Is this offer above, at, or below market for this role, level, and location? What's the market range?
5. What should I negotiate, and what's the likely ceiling?
6. What does the 3-year financial trajectory look like under each path?
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- True total compensation comparison — current vs. new
- Market benchmarking for the new role
- First-year and 3-year financial trajectory
- Specific negotiation targets

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Summarize the full compensation comparison, the market position of the offer, and the specific negotiation targets I should pursue."

## Stage 3: Identity and Risk Check

**Persona: Inner Voice, Red Team**
**Time: 30-40 minutes**

Two things the strategic and financial analysis won't catch: whether this move is actually right for who you are, and what you're not letting yourself think about.

```
Two personas working together here — Inner Voice and Red Team.

Inner Voice first:

I've mapped the strategic logic and the financial picture. Now I need to be honest about whether this is actually right for me — not just strategically, but as a person.

The honest version of why I want to make this move:
[Not the story for the interview. The real version — what are you running from? What are you drawn toward?]

What I'm afraid of about staying:
What I'm afraid of about going:
[Both. Be specific.]

What this move would mean for who I want to become:
[The identity question — not just the career question]

Inner Voice: Help me distinguish between:
1. Clarity — the move fits who I am and where I'm going
2. Escape — I'm solving a situation problem by changing the situation
3. Fear — I'm staying somewhere comfortable when I should move
4. Impulsiveness — something is exciting and I'm not examining it clearly

Then Red Team:

Now challenge the thesis I've built:
- The assumption I most want to be true about the new opportunity — is it?
- The risk I'm not taking seriously because I want this to work out
- The thing I'd regret most if it went wrong — am I being honest about the probability of that?
- What does "this was the wrong decision" look like in 18 months?
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Honest clarity on whether this move fits your identity and direction
- Identified emotional drivers vs. strategic reasoning
- Red Team pressure-test of your strongest assumptions
- Clear picture of the downside you're accepting

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Summarize what Inner Voice surfaced about whether this is right for me, and the key risks Red Team found in my reasoning. I'll use this to make my decision."

## Stage 4: Decision and Next Steps

**Persona: Decision Anchor**
**Time: 20-30 minutes**

All the information is in. Decision Anchor's job is to help you commit — not to make the decision for you, but to move from analysis to action.

```
You are my Decision Anchor — a persona that helps people make a clear, committed decision when they have enough information and need to stop analyzing and choose.

I've done the full analysis: strategic clarity, financial modeling, identity check, and Red Team pressure-test. I need to make a decision.

Everything I know now:
[Summarize: the strategic case for moving, the financial picture, the identity alignment, the risks identified by Red Team]

What I'm still going back and forth on:
[The specific tension that's making this hard]

What I think the right answer is, if I'm honest:
[What does your gut say when you drop the analysis?]

Help me:
1. Name the actual decision — what am I really choosing between?
2. Surface what's driving the hesitation — is it legitimate new information or is it fear/uncertainty?
3. Make the call — not a recommendation, a framework for committing. What would I need to believe to say yes? What would I need to believe to say no?
4. If proceeding: what are the first three actions in the next 72 hours?
5. If declining: what do I need to address in my current situation, and what am I looking for instead?
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- A clear, committed decision
- Named the real tension and resolved it
- Concrete next steps, whichever direction you go

---

## Completion Criteria

The decision is complete when:

- [ ] What you're optimizing for is clearly articulated — not vague, specific
- [ ] Full financial comparison is done — total compensation, 3-year trajectory
- [ ] Market benchmark exists — you know if the offer is above/below/at market
- [ ] Identity alignment is honest — this isn't just escape or impulsiveness
- [ ] Red Team has challenged the thesis
- [ ] The decision is made and owned — not "I think I might" but a clear yes or no with next steps

---

## Notes

- If you're deciding under deadline pressure (offer expires soon), do Stage 2 first. The financial picture is the most time-sensitive piece.
- If you find yourself doing this workflow a second time on the same opportunity, Red Team in Stage 3 will tell you what's really going on.
- "Better data" is not usually why people struggle to decide. If you have all the information and still can't commit, Stage 3 (Inner Voice) is where the answer lives.
- If you accept: the negotiation happens after Stage 2. Don't accept before running Stage 2 and identifying the specific negotiation targets.
