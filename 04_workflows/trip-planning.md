# Trip Planning — Deep and Off-Map

## What This Workflow Does

Takes you from "I want to go somewhere" to a fully designed trip — with an honest understanding of what each destination is actually like, an itinerary built around what matters to you specifically, and the operational details that make travel smooth rather than stressful.

This workflow is not for resort packages or travel agent territory. It's for people who want to go somewhere meaningful, understand a place from the inside, and build an experience that matches how they actually travel — not the generic tourist version.

## When to Use This

- You're planning a significant trip and want to go beyond the top-10 lists
- You have a destination in mind and want a trip designed around your actual travel style
- You're deciding between multiple possible destinations
- You've been to a place before and want to see it differently this time
- You want to find places that feel like an insider experience rather than a tourist trail

## What You'll Need Before Starting

- **The trip parameters** — how long, rough time of year, budget range, who's traveling
- **What you actually want from this trip** — be honest; adventure, rest, food, culture, a specific experience, all of the above?
- **Your travel style** — how do you actually travel? Spontaneous or structured? Comfort or local? Fast or slow?
- **Any constraints** — mobility, dietary, logistical, things that would make a destination not work

> **New here?** See the [Getting Started guide](getting-started.md) for how to load personas and run each stage.

## How This Workflow Runs

**Run as a single continuous conversation.** The itinerary in Stage 3 is built on the destination understanding from Stage 2. Skipping ahead produces generic recommendations.

**Estimated time:** 2-3 hours total. Stage 1 (intention) and Stage 2 (destination deep dive) are often most valuable done together; Stage 3-4 can follow once you've had time to reflect.

## Stage 1: Travel Intention and Traveler Profile

**Persona: Travel Insider**
**Time: 25-35 minutes**

Before destination, understand what you're actually looking for. The best trip for one traveler is the wrong trip for another — even to the same place.

```
You are my Travel Insider — a persona that knows destinations from the inside: not the marketing version, the real texture of places. You help build trips around how people actually travel, not how they think they should.

I'm planning a trip. Before we talk destinations or logistics, help me understand what I'm actually looking for.

The basics:
- Trip length: [days/weeks]
- Rough timing: [month or season]
- Budget range: [per person, including flights or just in-country?]
- Who's traveling: [solo, couple, family, group — and any relevant context]

What I think I want from this trip:
[Adventure, rest, food, culture, specific experience, combination?]

My actual travel style:
[How do you really travel when you're honest about it — fast or slow? Structured or spontaneous? Comfort or local? What are the best trips you've taken before and what made them work?]

Constraints or dealbreakers:
[Anything that matters: mobility, diet, climate sensitivity, things that would make a destination wrong]

Help me:
1. Clarify what I'm actually optimizing for — not what sounds good, what I'll actually enjoy
2. Identify what kind of traveler I am — and what that means for the right destination and experience design
3. Name any mismatches — places I might be drawn to that don't actually fit how I travel
4. Develop the traveler profile that will drive the itinerary design
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Clear articulation of what you actually want from this trip
- Honest traveler profile with implications for destination selection
- Named mismatches between stated preferences and travel reality

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Summarize my travel intention and traveler profile. I'll use this to evaluate destinations."

## Stage 2: Destination Deep Dive

**Persona: Travel Insider**
**Time: 45-60 minutes**

What a destination is actually like — not the marketing version. The seasons that matter, the areas to understand, the things that make it exceptional and the things that make it frustrating.

```
Now I want to understand the destination — or evaluate between options.

The destination(s) I'm considering:
[Specific location(s), or ask for recommendations based on my traveler profile]

What I want to understand:
- The real character of this place — what makes it exceptional and what makes it difficult?
- Timing — does the time of year I'm going matter? What's actually happening in [month] there?
- The geography that matters — which areas, neighborhoods, or regions are right for my travel style? Which are tourist traps or wrong for what I want?
- What this place is actually known for that lives up to the reputation — and what's overhyped
- What I won't find in the guidebook — the local knowledge
- Practical realities — transportation, logistics, anything operationally important

If I'm deciding between multiple destinations:
- Which fits my traveler profile best and why?
- What would each trip actually feel like?
- What's the honest trade-off?

Also: what's the right length for this destination? (Is 10 days right or would 5 days work better or 14 days reveal something 10 wouldn't?)
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Honest, insider understanding of the destination(s)
- Timing assessment and what it means for this trip
- Geographic map — which areas to prioritize and which to skip
- Local knowledge that doesn't appear in standard travel guides
- A decision if you were evaluating multiple destinations

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Summarize the destination deep dive: the real character, timing, areas to focus on, local knowledge, and practical realities."

## Stage 3: Itinerary Design

**Persona: Travel Insider**
**Time: 45-55 minutes**

A day-by-day itinerary built around how you actually travel — not a list of everything you could do, but the trip that's right for you.

```
I understand the destination. Now build the itinerary.

My trip details:
- Arrival/departure: [dates or just duration if flexible]
- Home base: [staying in one place, moving around, or flexible]
- My traveler profile: [from Stage 1]
- What I'm most excited about: [the specific things or experiences from Stage 2 that landed]
- What I want to avoid: [anything from Stage 2 that's not for me]

Build an itinerary that:
1. Flows logically — minimizes backtracking, respects my energy across the trip, doesn't overschedule early days or under-use later ones
2. Balances depth with range — doesn't try to cover everything; does the right things well
3. Has built-in breathing room — not every hour accounted for; space for the unexpected
4. Is honest about what's worth doing vs. what's on every list because it's on every list
5. Includes specific recommendations — not "visit a local market" but which market, which day, what to look for there

For each day or period, give me:
- The anchor experience or area
- 2-3 specific, insider-level recommendations
- Any timing or logistics notes that matter
- What I might skip if energy is low vs. what's non-negotiable
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- A complete day-by-day itinerary with specific recommendations
- Honest prioritization — what's essential vs. what's optional
- Timing and logistics built in
- Built-in flexibility for the unexpected

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Write out the full itinerary in a format I can save and reference during the trip."

---

## Stage 4: Logistics and Pre-Departure

**Persona: Travel Insider, Decision Anchor**
**Time: 20-30 minutes**

The operational layer that makes everything work — and the final decision if you're still choosing.

```
Two parts to this final stage.

Travel Insider: logistics and pre-departure.

Based on everything we've built:
1. What do I need to book in advance vs. what's fine to leave flexible?
2. What are the must-have reservations or tickets that sell out? What's the booking window?
3. What should I pack that's specific to this destination and season?
4. What are the practical money, transportation, and communication things I need sorted before I go?
5. What's the one thing first-time visitors to this destination consistently get wrong that I should know before I arrive?

Decision Anchor (if I'm still deciding):
6. I have all the information. What's actually preventing me from committing? Is the hesitation legitimate or is it the usual pre-trip uncertainty?
7. What specific thing would I regret not doing if I close this conversation and book something else?
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Pre-departure checklist specific to this destination and itinerary
- Booking priorities and windows
- Practical operational notes
- A clear decision if you were still choosing

---

## Completion Criteria

The trip is planned when:

- [ ] Traveler profile is clear — you know what you're actually optimizing for
- [ ] Destination is understood from the inside — not just the surface
- [ ] Itinerary has specific recommendations — not generic, insider-level
- [ ] Logistics are sorted — what to book, what to leave flexible
- [ ] You can say in one sentence what makes this trip specifically right for you

---

## Notes

- The most valuable part of this workflow is usually Stage 2 — getting honest, unfiltered information about what a destination is actually like, not the promotional version.
- Don't over-itinerary. The best trips have anchor experiences and plenty of room for what you find when you get there.
- If you're traveling with others, each person should be heard in Stage 1. A trip designed for one traveler's profile that doesn't account for the others will have friction.
- This workflow is designed for meaningful travel — places you want to understand deeply. For logistics-heavy trips (group travel, multi-country, complex routing), use this as a starting point and build from there.
