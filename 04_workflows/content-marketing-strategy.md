# Content and Marketing Strategy Workflow

## What This Workflow Does

Takes you from "we need a content strategy" to a complete, actionable marketing and content plan — anchored in a specific audience, a clear message, and channels where you can actually win.

Most content strategies fail because they're built on what the brand wants to say rather than what the audience needs to hear, and on too many channels to execute well. This workflow builds from the audience inward, establishes a clear voice and message, and defines a focused channel strategy that a real team can actually execute.

## When to Use This

- You're building a content or marketing strategy from scratch
- Your existing content isn't driving meaningful results and you want to diagnose why
- You're launching a new product, service, or business and need a marketing foundation
- You want to consolidate scattered content efforts into a coherent system
- You're a founder, solo marketer, or small team trying to punch above your weight

## What You'll Need Before Starting

- **What you're marketing** — the product, service, or brand, described honestly
- **What you know about your audience** — who they are, what they care about, where they spend time
- **Your resources** — team size, content budget, time available per week for content creation
- **Current state** — what you're already doing, what's working, what isn't

> **New here?** See the [Getting Started guide](getting-started.md) for how to load personas and run each stage.

## How This Workflow Runs

**Run as a single continuous conversation.** Stage 3 (channel strategy) won't be right without Stage 2's audience definition. Don't skip stages.

**Estimated time:** 3-4 hours. Most teams split after Stage 3 and return for Stage 4-5 once they've had a day to review.

## Stage 1: Audience and Positioning Foundation

**Persona: Marketing Mastermind**
**Time: 40-50 minutes**

Marketing strategy is an audience-first exercise. Everything flows from a precise understanding of who you're trying to reach, what they actually care about, and why they'd pay attention to you. This stage builds that foundation.

```
You are my Marketing Mastermind — a persona that builds marketing strategy from the audience inward, with a ruthless focus on what actually moves buyers.

I'm building a content and marketing strategy. Before we talk about channels or content types, help me get clear on the foundation.

What I'm marketing:
[Describe the product, service, or brand — what it is and what problem it solves]

My best current understanding of my audience:
[Who they are — be as specific as possible. Job title, situation, what they struggle with, what they're trying to achieve]

How I'm currently positioned vs. alternatives:
[What makes you different? Why would someone choose you over the obvious alternatives — including doing nothing?]

What success looks like in 12 months:
[Leads, revenue, brand awareness, audience size — whatever's most important for your situation]

Help me:
1. Sharpen the audience definition — who specifically feels the pain most acutely? Not demographics, the situation they're in.
2. Define the positioning in one sentence — what you do, for whom, that's different because of what specific reason?
3. Identify the message hierarchy — what does the audience need to believe to buy? In what order do they need to believe it?
4. Find the insight — what does your audience know that most brands don't say out loud? What's the true thing about their situation that would make them feel heard?
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Sharpened audience definition — a specific situation, not demographics
- Positioning statement in one sentence
- Message hierarchy — what beliefs you need to build, in order
- The core audience insight that will anchor your content

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Summarize the audience definition, positioning statement, message hierarchy, and the core insight. I'll use this to build the content strategy."

## Stage 2: Brand Voice and Content Principles

**Persona: Storyteller, Marketing Mastermind**
**Time: 30-40 minutes**

Strategy without voice is an empty framework. This stage defines how you sound — specifically enough that anyone creating content for you could follow it.

```
You are my Storyteller and Marketing Mastermind — help me define the voice and content principles that will make my content recognizable and consistent.

What I know about my positioning and audience (from Stage 1):
[Summary of audience definition and positioning]

My honest read on how I sound today:
[Is current content formal or casual? Confident or hedged? Expert or accessible? What's working and what feels off?]

Brands or creators whose voice I admire:
[Name 1-3 examples and why — not to copy, for calibration]

What I want people to feel when they encounter my content:
[The emotional impression — trusted advisor? Practical expert? Refreshing contrarian? Peer who gets it?]

Help me define:
1. Voice principles (3-5): specific characteristics of how I write and speak — not "clear and concise," the specific qualities that make the voice distinct
2. What I never do: 3-5 things my brand consistently avoids in tone, framing, or content approach
3. The editorial POV: what perspective does my brand take on the world? What do I believe that my audience hasn't fully articulated yet?
4. Voice examples: write one paragraph in my brand voice about my core topic — show me what this sounds like in practice
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- 3-5 specific voice principles with guardrails
- Clear "never do" list
- Defined editorial point of view
- A working example of the voice in practice

## Stage 3: Channel Strategy and Focus

**Persona: Pattern Seer, Marketing Mastermind**
**Time: 35-45 minutes**

Most teams are spread across too many channels. This stage identifies where your audience actually pays attention, what channels match your resources, and builds a focused strategy around 1-2 channels to own.

```
You are my Pattern Seer and Marketing Mastermind — help me identify the channels where I can actually win given my audience, resources, and goals.

My audience and positioning (from Stage 1):
[Summary]

My resources:
[Team size, time available per week for content, budget for distribution]

What I'm currently doing:
[Channels, posting frequency, what's getting engagement vs. crickets]

Where my audience actually spends time:
[What you know or believe about where they consume content]

Help me:
1. Map the channel landscape for this audience — where do they actually go to find information, learn, and discover new tools/services like mine?
2. Evaluate my current channels honestly — where are we getting real traction vs. going through the motions?
3. Identify the 1-2 channels to own — where can I realistically be excellent with my resources? What would "owning" each channel look like?
4. Build the content engine — what's the minimum viable content system that sustains these channels? (Frequency, format, production requirements)
5. Define the amplification strategy — how does content from owned channels reach new audiences? (SEO, paid, partnerships, distribution)
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Clear-eyed assessment of your current channel performance
- 1-2 channels identified to own with justification
- Minimum viable content engine for those channels
- Amplification strategy for reach beyond existing audience

**Optional checkpoint:**
> "Summarize the channel strategy: which 1-2 channels to own, what that looks like operationally, and the amplification approach."

## Stage 4: Content Plan

**Persona: Marketing Mastermind, Storyteller**
**Time: 35-45 minutes**

Strategy becomes execution here. This stage builds the actual content plan — specific enough to hand to a creator or follow yourself.

```
I have my audience, positioning, voice, and channel strategy. Now help me build the actual content plan.

The channels I'm focusing on:
[From Stage 3]

My publishing capacity:
[How often can I realistically publish on each channel?]

The message hierarchy I'm building toward:
[From Stage 1 — the sequence of beliefs I need to create]

Help me build:
1. Content pillars (3-4): the recurring themes I'll cover that map to my message hierarchy and audience interests — specific enough to generate dozens of ideas from each
2. Content formats: what formats work best on my chosen channels and match my production capacity?
3. The editorial calendar framework: what does a consistent month of content look like? (Not 30 specific topics, a repeatable structure I can fill each month)
4. The lead magnet or anchor content: what's the one piece of substantial content that would bring in the right audience and demonstrate my depth? (Guide, tool, framework, original research)
5. 15 specific content ideas: across my pillars, for my chosen channels, tailored to my audience insight from Stage 1
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- 3-4 content pillars with definitions
- Monthly editorial calendar framework
- Lead magnet / anchor content concept
- 15 specific content ideas ready to execute

## Stage 5: Red Team and Launch Plan

**Persona: Red Team, Marketing Mastermind**
**Time: 25-35 minutes**

Challenge the strategy before committing to executing it — then build the 30-day launch plan.

```
Red Team first: challenge my content and marketing strategy.

My strategy summary:
[Audience, positioning, channels, content approach — brief summary of what I've built]

What I'm most confident about:
What I'm least sure about:

Find:
1. The assumption I'm most likely wrong about — about the audience, the channel, or the message
2. The strategy that looks good on paper but won't survive contact with real execution — what breaks?
3. What I'm missing that my best competitor already understands about this market
4. Where my strategy is generic enough that it could belong to any brand in my category

Then Marketing Mastermind: build the 30-day launch plan.
5. What do I execute in the first 30 days to test whether this strategy is working?
6. What are the specific metrics that tell me at day 30 whether to continue, adjust, or change direction?
7. What's the first piece of content, ready to publish, that best represents this strategy?
```

**What you'll have at the end of this stage:**
- Red Team pressure-test of the strategy
- Identified assumptions and gaps
- Concrete 30-day execution plan
- Success metrics at day 30

---

## Completion Criteria

Your content and marketing strategy is ready to execute when:

- [ ] Audience is defined specifically — a situation, not demographics
- [ ] Positioning is clear — one sentence that differentiates you
- [ ] Voice is defined — specific enough that someone else could follow it
- [ ] Channels are focused — 1-2 to own, not 6 to dabble in
- [ ] Content pillars are defined — recurring themes tied to message hierarchy
- [ ] Editorial calendar framework exists — repeatable monthly structure
- [ ] Red Team has challenged it — no unchallenged assumptions
- [ ] 30-day launch plan is specific — first 30 days are mapped with success metrics

---

## Notes

- The most common mistake is trying to be on too many channels. Every channel you add dilutes the quality of every other channel.
- If you have no audience data, Stage 1's audience definition is your best-current-hypothesis. Plan to validate it in the first 30 days.
- The voice section (Stage 2) is where most strategies stay generic. Specificity is the difference between content that sounds like everyone else and content that's recognizable.
