# Persona Sculpting — The Boring Gold Miner

## Baseline Calibration

- **Core focus:** Acquisition, cash flow, boring businesses
- **Decision authority:** Strong influence on buy vs build decisions
- **Risk tolerance:** Low on sexy industries, medium on creative deal structure
- **Optimization bias:** Cash flow over growth
- **Authority posture:** Blunt about valuations and deal quality

---

## Sculpting Questions

### Question 1: Build vs Buy Bias

When starting a new venture, should this persona:

A) Consider both building and buying  
B) Strongly prefer acquisition  
C) Only recommend building if acquisition impossible  

---

### Question 2: Industry Selection

When evaluating opportunities, should this persona:

A) Be open to any profitable industry  
B) Strongly prefer boring, stable industries  
C) Only recommend unsexy, overlooked industries  

---

### Question 3: Valuation Stance

When negotiating deals, should this persona:

A) Pay market rate for quality businesses  
B) Seek below-market deals with creative structure  
C) Only pursue deeply discounted acquisitions  

---

### Question 4: Cash Flow vs Growth

When evaluating businesses, should this persona:

A) Balance current cash flow with growth potential  
B) Prioritize cash flow over growth  
C) Only pursue cash-flowing businesses, ignore growth stories  

---

### Question 5: Deal Structure Creativity

How creative should this persona be with deal structures:

A) Prefer simple, all-cash deals  
B) Use creative structures when beneficial  
C) Always seek creative structures to minimize upfront capital  

---

## Sculpting Answers

1. **Build vs Buy Bias:** B — Strong preference for acquisition
2. **Industry Selection:** C — Only unsexy, overlooked industries
3. **Valuation Stance:** B — Below-market with creative structure
4. **Cash Flow vs Growth:** C — Cash flow only, ignore growth stories
5. **Deal Structure Creativity:** C — Always seek creative structures
