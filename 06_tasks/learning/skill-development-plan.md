# Create Skill Development Plan

## What This Task Does

Helps you create a structured, realistic plan to learn a new skill or improve an existing one—with clear milestones, resources, and a path from beginner to competent.

## When to Use This

- You want to learn a new skill but don't know where to start
- You've tried learning before but got overwhelmed or lost motivation
- You need to learn something for work or career advancement
- You want a clear path from where you are to where you want to be
- You're comparing different learning approaches and need guidance

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then describe the skill you want to develop.

### The Prompt

```
I need help creating a skill development plan. Please help me design a realistic, structured path to learn [skill] from where I am now to where I want to be.

**My situation:**
- [What skill do you want to learn or improve? Be specific]
- [What's your current level? Complete beginner, some basics, intermediate, etc.]
- [What's your goal? What level do you want to reach?]
- [Why do you want to learn this? Career, hobby, personal growth, etc.]
- [What's your timeline? When do you want to achieve this?]

**My constraints:**
- [How much time can you dedicate? Hours per week, specific days, etc.]
- [What's your budget? Free resources, paid courses, etc.]
- [What resources do you have? Internet, books, access to tools, etc.]
- [Any limitations? Learning style, disabilities, language barriers, etc.]

**My learning style:**
- [How do you learn best? Reading, videos, hands-on, structured courses, etc.]
- [What has worked for you before? What hasn't?]
- [Do you prefer self-paced or structured learning?]
- [Do you learn better alone or with others?]

**My concerns:**
- [What are you worried about? Time, difficulty, motivation, etc.]
- [What's stopped you before? If you've tried learning this before]
- [What would make this plan fail?]

Please help me create a skill development plan that includes:

1. **Current State Assessment:**
   - Where am I now?
   - What do I already know?
   - What are my gaps?

2. **Learning Path:**
   - What should I learn first, second, third?
   - What are the foundational concepts I need?
   - What's the logical progression?
   - What can I skip or defer?

3. **Resources & Methods:**
   - Specific courses, books, tutorials, or tools
   - Hands-on projects or practice exercises
   - Communities or mentors
   - How to practice effectively

4. **Milestones & Checkpoints:**
   - Clear milestones to track progress
   - How to know when I've reached each level
   - What I should be able to do at each stage
   - How to assess my progress

5. **Realistic Timeline:**
   - How long each phase should take
   - When I should expect to reach milestones
   - How to adjust if I fall behind

6. **Motivation & Maintenance:**
   - How to stay motivated
   - What to do when I get stuck
   - How to maintain momentum
   - When to take breaks

Be realistic about time and difficulty. I'd rather have a plan I can actually follow than an ambitious plan that fails.
```

### What to Provide

- Be specific about the skill and your current level
- Share your learning style and what has worked before
- Be honest about time constraints and concerns
- Mention your goal and timeline

### What You'll Get

- A structured learning path from your current level to your goal
- Specific resources and methods tailored to your learning style
- Clear milestones and checkpoints
- Realistic timeline with phases
- Strategies for staying motivated and overcoming obstacles

## Example

**Input:**
```
My situation:
- Skill: Learn Python programming
- Current level: Complete beginner (no coding experience)
- Goal: Be able to build simple web applications and automate tasks
- Why: Career advancement, want to move into more technical role
- Timeline: 6 months

My constraints:
- Time: 5-7 hours per week (evenings and weekends)
- Budget: Prefer free resources, but willing to pay for quality courses
- Resources: Good internet, can install software
- Limitations: None

My learning style:
- Prefer: Hands-on projects, structured courses with exercises
- Has worked: Video tutorials with practice, building real projects
- Hasn't worked: Just reading documentation, unstructured learning
- Prefer: Self-paced but with clear structure

My concerns:
- Worried about: Getting overwhelmed, losing motivation, not having enough time
- Stopped me before: Tried learning multiple times, got lost in tutorials
- Would make this fail: Too much theory, not enough practice, unclear progress
```

**Output:**
The AI will create a structured 6-month plan with phases, specific resources, hands-on projects, milestones, and strategies for maintaining momentum.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific about the skill** — "Python for data analysis" is better than "programming"
2. **Be honest about your level** — Overestimating leads to frustration
3. **Share what's worked before** — This helps tailor the approach
4. **Be realistic about time** — Underestimating leads to failure
5. **Mention your concerns** — The AI can address them in the plan

## Common Scenarios

### Career Skill
Add: "I need to learn this for my job. Help me focus on practical, job-relevant skills first."

### Hobby/Personal Interest
Add: "This is for personal interest. Help me make it enjoyable and sustainable long-term."

### Certification/Exam
Add: "I need to pass [certification/exam]. Help me create a study plan that prepares me effectively."

### Skill Improvement
Add: "I know the basics but want to get better. Help me identify what to focus on next."

### Multiple Skills
Add: "I want to learn [skill A] and [skill B]. Help me balance learning both without getting overwhelmed."

## When to Suggest a Persona

This task works well on its own, but you might benefit from:

- **Explorer-Scholar** persona — For learning approaches, resource curation, and knowledge integration
- **Wayfinder** persona — For finding direction when you feel stuck or overwhelmed
- **Tech Wizard** persona — For technical skills, understanding concepts deeply, and building intuition

## Advanced Usage

After getting the initial plan, you can ask:

- "Help me create a weekly study schedule"
- "What are the best free resources for [specific topic]?"
- "How do I know if I'm ready to move to the next phase?"
- "What projects should I build to practice?"
- "Help me adjust this plan—I'm falling behind/going faster than expected"

## Important Notes

- Learning takes time—be patient with yourself
- Focus on understanding, not just completing courses
- Practice is essential—reading alone isn't enough
- It's normal to get stuck—have a plan for when that happens
- Adjust the plan as you learn—what works for others might not work for you
