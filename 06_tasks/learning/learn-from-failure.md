# Learn from Failure or Mistake

## What This Task Does

Helps you extract real learning from failures, mistakes, or setbacks by analyzing what happened, understanding root causes, identifying patterns, and creating actionable insights—turning failure into valuable experience.

## When to Use This

- Something didn't work out and you want to understand why
- You made a mistake and want to learn from it
- A project failed and you want to extract lessons
- You keep making similar mistakes and want to break the pattern
- You want to turn a setback into growth

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then describe what happened.

### The Prompt

```
I want to learn from [failure/mistake/setback]. Please help me understand what happened, why it happened, and what I should do differently.

**What happened:**
- [Describe what happened. Be specific and factual]
- [What was the outcome? What went wrong?]
- [When did you realize something was wrong?]
- [What was the impact? On you, others, the project, etc.?]

**The context:**
- [What were you trying to achieve? What was the goal?]
- [What was the situation? Timeline, constraints, resources, etc.]
- [Who was involved? Just you, team, others?]
- [What was your role? What were you responsible for?]

**What you did:**
- [What actions did you take? What decisions did you make?]
- [What did you assume? What did you think would happen?]
- [What information did you have? What were you missing?]
- [What did you prioritize? What did you deprioritize?]

**Your concerns:**
- [What are you worried about? Repeating the mistake, impact on others, etc.]
- [What questions do you have? What don't you understand?]
- [What feels unclear about what happened?]
- [What would help you move forward?]

Please help me:

1. **Understand what actually happened:**
   - What were the facts vs. assumptions?
   - What was the sequence of events?
   - When did things start going wrong?
   - What were the contributing factors?

2. **Identify root causes:**
   - Why did this happen? (Not just what, but why)
   - What assumptions were wrong?
   - What information was missing?
   - What decisions led to this outcome?
   - What was outside your control vs. what could you have influenced?

3. **Extract lessons:**
   - What did you learn? Be specific
   - What patterns do you see? (Is this part of a larger pattern?)
   - What should you do differently next time?
   - What should you keep doing? (Not everything was wrong)
   - What early warning signs did you miss?

4. **Create actionable insights:**
   - What specific changes will you make?
   - What systems or processes would prevent this?
   - What questions should you ask earlier?
   - What information do you need to gather next time?
   - How do you recognize this pattern earlier?

5. **Move forward:**
   - How do you prevent this from happening again?
   - What do you need to do to fix or address the current situation?
   - How do you rebuild trust or repair relationships? (If applicable)
   - What's the next step?

Be honest and constructive. I want real learning, not just reassurance or blame.
```

### What to Provide

- Describe what happened factually and specifically
- Share the context and your role
- Explain what you did and what you assumed
- Be honest about your concerns and questions

### What You'll Get

- Clear understanding of what happened and why
- Root cause analysis (not just surface symptoms)
- Specific lessons and insights
- Actionable changes to make
- A path forward

## Example

**Input:**
```
What happened:
- Outcome: Missed a project deadline, had to ask for extension
- Realized: 2 days before deadline that I was way behind
- Impact: Disappointed client, had to rush final work, quality suffered

The context:
- Goal: Complete website redesign project
- Situation: 4-week timeline, working part-time, other commitments
- Involved: Just me (solo project)
- My role: Designer and developer, full responsibility

What I did:
- Actions: Started with design, spent too long perfecting, coding started late
- Assumed: I had more time than I did, could catch up later
- Information: Had the timeline, but didn't track progress
- Prioritized: Making design perfect, deprioritized: Time management

My concerns:
- Worried: Will repeat this, client lost trust
- Questions: How do I estimate time better? How do I avoid perfectionism?
- Unclear: Was the timeline unrealistic or was I just bad at managing it?
- Need: Better system for future projects
```

**Output:**
The AI will help you understand what happened, identify root causes (perfectionism, poor time tracking), extract lessons, and create actionable changes.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be honest** — Don't minimize or exaggerate what happened
2. **Focus on facts** — What actually happened, not what you wish happened
3. **Look for patterns** — Is this part of a larger pattern?
4. **Be specific** — "I didn't track time" is better than "I was disorganized"
5. **Focus on learning** — Not blame or excuses, but understanding

## Common Scenarios

### Project Failure
Add: "A project I was leading failed. Help me understand what went wrong and how to prevent it."

### Repeated Mistake
Add: "I keep making the same type of mistake. Help me understand the pattern and break it."

### Relationship Impact
Add: "My mistake affected others. Help me understand what happened and how to repair trust."

### Career Setback
Add: "I had a career setback. Help me learn from it and move forward constructively."

## When to Suggest a Persona

This task works well on its own, but you might benefit from:

- **Red Team** persona — For identifying risks, failure modes, and stress-testing your analysis
- **Pattern Seer** persona — For recognizing patterns, themes, and long-term implications

## Advanced Usage

After the initial analysis, you can ask:

- "Help me create a system to prevent this from happening again"
- "What are the early warning signs I should watch for?"
- "How do I rebuild trust after this mistake?"
- "What questions should I ask myself before similar situations?"
- "Help me turn this into a strength—what did I learn that's valuable?"

## Important Notes

- Failure is data, not identity—learn from it without self-blame
- Look for root causes, not just symptoms—why did this happen?
- Focus on what you can control—some factors are outside your influence
- Extract lessons, not just explanations—what will you do differently?
- Move forward—learning from failure means applying the lessons
