# Create Habit Formation System

## What This Task Does

Helps you design a sustainable system for building new habits or breaking old ones by understanding your patterns, creating the right environment, and establishing realistic systems that actually work long-term.

## When to Use This

- You want to build a new habit but keep failing
- You've tried multiple times and want a different approach
- You want to break a bad habit but don't know how
- You want to build multiple habits and need a system
- You want to understand why previous attempts failed

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then describe the habit you want to build or break.

### The Prompt

```
I need help creating a system to [build/break] the habit of [habit]. Please help me design something that actually works long-term, not just for a few weeks.

**My situation:**
- [What habit do you want to build or break? Be specific]
- [Why is this important to you? What's the deeper motivation?]
- [How many times have you tried before? What happened?]
- [What usually causes you to fail or give up?]
- [What's your current situation? Schedule, environment, constraints, etc.]

**My goals:**
- [What does success look like? Be specific and measurable]
- [What's your timeline? When do you want this habit established?]
- [How will you know it's working?]
- [What's the minimum viable version of this habit?]

**My constraints:**
- [What are your time constraints? When can you actually do this?]
- [What are your environmental constraints? Home, work, etc.]
- [What are your energy/mental constraints? When are you most capable?]
- [What resources do you have? Tools, support, money, etc.]

**My patterns:**
- [What times of day are you most successful?]
- [What triggers your current behavior (if breaking a habit)?]
- [What has worked for you before (even briefly)?]
- [What are your biggest obstacles?]

Please help me:

1. **Understand the habit deeply:**
   - Why do I want this? What's the real motivation?
   - What's the minimum viable version I can start with?
   - What are the common failure modes for this habit?
   - What makes this habit sustainable vs. unsustainable?

2. **Design the system:**
   - What's the smallest version I can start with?
   - How do I make it easier to do than not do?
   - What environmental changes do I need?
   - What triggers or cues will remind me?
   - How do I track progress without it becoming a burden?

3. **Plan for obstacles:**
   - What will cause me to fail? (Be honest)
   - How do I handle missed days without giving up?
   - What's my plan for when motivation is low?
   - How do I adjust when life gets chaotic?

4. **Create accountability:**
   - How do I track this without it feeling like a chore?
   - Who can support me? (If applicable)
   - What are the milestones I'm working toward?
   - How do I celebrate progress without getting complacent?

5. **Build sustainability:**
   - How does this habit become automatic?
   - When can I increase the difficulty?
   - How do I maintain this long-term?
   - What are the warning signs I'm about to quit?

Be realistic and practical. I'd rather have a small habit I can maintain than a big one I abandon.
```

### What to Provide

- Be specific about the habit and why it matters to you
- Be honest about past failures and what caused them
- Share your constraints and patterns honestly
- Describe your current situation and environment

### What You'll Get

- Deep understanding of why the habit matters and what makes it sustainable
- A practical system designed for your specific situation
- Obstacle planning and failure recovery strategies
- Accountability and tracking methods
- Long-term sustainability plan

## Example

**Input:**
```
My situation:
- Habit: Exercise regularly (currently exercise 0-1 times per week, want to do 4-5 times)
- Why: Want more energy, better health, feel stronger
- Tried before: Multiple times, usually lasts 2-3 weeks then stops
- What causes failure: Life gets busy, lose motivation, feels like a chore
- Current situation: Work full-time, have 2 hours free in evenings, home gym available

My goals:
- Success: Exercise 4-5 times per week, feel stronger, have more energy
- Timeline: Want to establish this over 3 months
- Know it's working: Consistent 4+ times per week for a month
- Minimum viable: Even 10 minutes counts, just need to show up

My constraints:
- Time: Evenings after work, weekends available
- Environment: Home gym, can also walk/run outside
- Energy: Tired after work, but have energy on weekends
- Resources: Home gym equipment, can afford gym membership if needed

My patterns:
- Most successful: Weekend mornings, but weekdays are hard
- Triggers: Stress makes me skip, feeling tired makes me skip
- What worked briefly: Starting small, having a workout buddy (moved away)
- Biggest obstacles: Tired after work, all-or-nothing thinking
```

**Output:**
The AI will design a system that accounts for your constraints, starts small, plans for obstacles, and builds sustainability.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be honest about failures** — Understanding why you failed helps design a better system
2. **Start smaller than you think** — "10 minutes counts" is better than "must do full workout"
3. **Focus on systems, not motivation** — Make it easier to do than not do
4. **Plan for failure** — Missing a day shouldn't mean giving up
5. **Understand your why** — Deep motivation sustains when motivation fades

## Common Scenarios

### Building a New Habit
Add: "I want to build [habit]. Help me start small and build gradually."

### Breaking a Bad Habit
Add: "I want to break [habit]. Help me understand my triggers and create alternatives."

### Multiple Habits
Add: "I want to build [X] habits. Help me prioritize and sequence them so I don't overwhelm myself."

### Habit That Failed Before
Add: "I've tried [habit] [X] times and failed. Help me understand why and design something different."

## When to Suggest a Persona

This task works well on its own, but you might benefit from:

- **Wayfinder** persona — For finding direction, understanding motivation, and aligning habits with values
- **Nurturing Steward** persona — For sustainable pacing, avoiding burnout, and maintaining long-term habits

## Advanced Usage

After getting the initial system, you can ask:

- "Help me create a tracking system for this habit"
- "What should I do when I miss a day?"
- "How do I know when to increase the difficulty?"
- "Help me design the environment to support this habit"
- "What are the warning signs I'm about to quit?"

## Important Notes

- Small and consistent beats big and sporadic
- Missing one day doesn't mean failure—systems account for this
- Environment matters more than willpower—design your environment
- Start smaller than feels necessary—you can always increase
- Focus on the system, not the outcome—trust the process
