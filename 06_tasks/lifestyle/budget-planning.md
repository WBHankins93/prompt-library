# Personal Budget Planning

## What This Task Does

Helps you create a realistic, sustainable budget that fits your actual life—not a theoretical budget you'll abandon. Focuses on understanding your spending, setting realistic goals, and creating a system you can actually maintain.

## When to Use This

- You want to get better control of your finances
- You're spending more than you make or want to save more
- You've tried budgeting before but it didn't work
- You want to understand where your money is going
- You have financial goals and need a plan to reach them

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then provide your financial situation.

### The Prompt

```
I need help creating a realistic budget that I can actually stick to. Please help me understand my finances and create a sustainable system.

**My situation:**
- [What's your monthly income? After taxes if possible]
- [What are your fixed expenses? Rent, utilities, insurance, etc.]
- [What are your variable expenses? Food, gas, entertainment, etc.]
- [Do you have debt? Credit cards, loans, etc.?]
- [Do you have savings? Emergency fund, retirement, etc.?]
- [What's your biggest financial concern?]

**My spending:**
- [Where does your money actually go? Be honest]
- [What do you spend too much on?]
- [What expenses surprise you?]
- [What can you not cut? What's non-negotiable?]
- [What could you reduce if needed?]

**My goals:**
- [What are your financial goals? Save for X, pay off debt, build emergency fund, etc.]
- [How much do you want to save per month?]
- [What's your timeline? Short-term vs. long-term goals?]
- [What would financial security look like for you?]

**My constraints:**
- [What's your actual lifestyle? Not your ideal, but your reality]
- [What expenses are fixed and can't change?]
- [What's your risk tolerance? Can you cut back significantly or need gradual changes?]
- [Any upcoming expenses? Big purchases, life changes, etc.?]

Please help me:

1. **Understand my current situation:**
   - Where is my money actually going?
   - What's my spending pattern?
   - What are the problem areas?
   - What's working well?

2. **Create a realistic budget:**
   - Fixed expenses (can't change)
   - Variable expenses (can adjust)
   - Savings goals
   - Fun money (realistic, not zero)
   - Emergency buffer

3. **Identify opportunities:**
   - Where can I save without major lifestyle changes?
   - What expenses can be reduced?
   - What can be eliminated?
   - What should I prioritize?

4. **Set up a system:**
   - How to track spending (simple method)
   - How to review and adjust monthly
   - How to handle unexpected expenses
   - How to stay motivated

5. **Plan for obstacles:**
   - What will cause me to abandon this budget?
   - How do I handle months when I overspend?
   - How do I adjust when income or expenses change?
   - What's my plan for staying on track?

Be realistic and non-judgmental. I want a budget that fits my actual life, not a perfect budget I'll abandon.
```

### What to Provide

- Be honest about your income and spending
- Share your actual expenses, not your ideal expenses
- Mention your financial goals and concerns
- Be realistic about what you can and can't change

### What You'll Get

- Clear understanding of where your money goes
- A realistic budget tailored to your situation
- Opportunities to save without major sacrifices
- A simple tracking and review system
- Strategies for staying on track

## Example

**Input:**
```
My situation:
- Monthly income: $4,500 after taxes
- Fixed expenses: $1,800 rent, $200 utilities, $150 insurance, $300 car payment
- Variable: Food $600, gas $200, entertainment $300, miscellaneous $400
- Debt: $5,000 credit card, $15,000 car loan
- Savings: $2,000 emergency fund
- Biggest concern: Not saving enough, credit card debt

My spending:
- Money goes to: Rent, food, entertainment, some impulse purchases
- Spend too much on: Eating out, online shopping
- Surprises me: How much I spend on "small" purchases that add up
- Can't cut: Rent, car payment, insurance
- Could reduce: Eating out, entertainment, impulse purchases

My goals:
- Pay off credit card debt (currently paying minimum)
- Build emergency fund to $10,000
- Save $500/month if possible
- Timeline: Pay off debt in 12-18 months

My constraints:
- Live in expensive city, rent is fixed
- Need car for work
- Like to have some fun money, but can cut back
- Can make gradual changes, don't need to cut everything at once
```

**Output:**
The AI will analyze your spending, create a realistic budget, identify savings opportunities, and help you create a system to pay off debt and build savings.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be completely honest** — Fudging numbers creates a budget that won't work
2. **Include everything** — Even small expenses add up
3. **Be realistic about lifestyle** — "I'll never eat out" usually fails
4. **Share your concerns** — "I'm worried about cutting too much" helps create balance
5. **Ask for alternatives** — "What are creative ways to save on [expense]?"

### Irregular Income
Add: "My income varies. Help me create a budget that works with inconsistent income."

### Major Life Change
Add: "I'm [getting married/having a baby/moving/changing jobs]. Help me adjust my budget."

After getting the initial budget, you can ask:

- "Help me create a debt payoff plan"
- "What are creative ways to save on [specific expense]?"
- "How do I handle unexpected expenses in my budget?"
- "Help me create a savings plan for [specific goal]"
- "What should I do with extra money when I have it?"

## Important Notes

- A realistic budget you follow beats a perfect budget you abandon
- Include fun money—deprivation leads to failure
- Review and adjust monthly—budgets aren't set in stone
- Start where you are, not where you wish you were
- Small changes compound—don't underestimate small savings
