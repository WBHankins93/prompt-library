# Major Purchase Decision

## What This Task Does

Helps you make informed purchase decisions by evaluating options, identifying tradeoffs, and considering long-term value—not just immediate cost.

## When to Use This

- You're considering a major purchase (car, house, expensive equipment, etc.)
- You're comparing multiple options and feeling overwhelmed
- You want to understand the true cost beyond the price tag
- You're unsure if you can afford something or if it's worth it
- You want to avoid buyer's remorse by thinking through the decision

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below and paste it into your AI assistant. Then provide details about your purchase decision.

### The Prompt

```
I need help making a major purchase decision. Please help me evaluate my options and think through the tradeoffs.

**My situation:**
- [What are you considering buying? Be specific]
- [What's your budget or price range?]
- [What's driving this purchase? Need, want, upgrade, etc.]
- [How urgent is this? Can you wait, or do you need it soon?]

**The options:**
[Describe your options - specific products, models, brands, etc. Include prices if you know them]

OR if you're still researching:
- [What are you looking for? Features, requirements, etc.]
- [What's your ideal vs. minimum requirements?]
- [What are you willing to compromise on?]

**My constraints:**
- [Financial: Budget limits, payment options, etc.]
- [Practical: Space, compatibility, maintenance, etc.]
- [Timeline: When do you need this?]
- [Other: Any other considerations?]

**My concerns:**
- [What are you worried about? Cost, quality, buyer's remorse, etc.]
- [What mistakes have you made with similar purchases?]
- [What would make this purchase a mistake?]

Please help me:

1. **Evaluate each option:**
   - Pros and cons
   - True cost (including maintenance, accessories, etc.)
   - Long-term value
   - Risk factors

2. **Identify tradeoffs:**
   - What am I gaining vs. losing with each option?
   - What's the cost of waiting vs. buying now?
   - What's the cost of buying cheap vs. expensive?

3. **Consider alternatives:**
   - Is there a better option I haven't considered?
   - Should I wait, buy used, or look elsewhere?
   - Are there ways to reduce cost without sacrificing too much?

4. **Make a recommendation:**
   - Which option makes the most sense for my situation?
   - Why this option over the others?
   - What should I do next?

Be honest about risks and tradeoffs. I'd rather hear hard truths than make a bad decision.
```

### What to Provide

- Be specific about what you're buying and your options
- Share your budget and constraints honestly
- Mention your concerns and past mistakes (if relevant)
- Include any research you've already done

### What You'll Get

- Detailed evaluation of each option
- Clear tradeoff analysis
- Alternative suggestions
- A recommendation with reasoning
- Next steps

## Example

**Input:**
```
My situation:
- Considering: New laptop for work
- Budget: $1,500-$2,000
- Driving this: Current laptop is 5 years old and slowing down
- Urgency: Not urgent, but would like to upgrade soon

The options:
- Option 1: MacBook Pro 14" ($1,999) - High-end, great for my work
- Option 2: MacBook Air 15" ($1,299) - Cheaper, still good performance
- Option 3: Wait 6 months for new models (prices may drop)

My constraints:
- Need it for video editing and design work
- Prefer Mac ecosystem (have other Apple devices)
- Can pay cash or finance
- Need it to last 4-5 years

My concerns:
- Spending too much when a cheaper option might work
- Buying right before new models come out
- Not getting enough value for the money
```

**Output:**
The AI will evaluate each option, analyze tradeoffs, consider alternatives, and provide a recommendation based on your specific needs and constraints.

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific about your needs** — "Video editing" is better than "work stuff"
2. **Share your actual budget** — Not your ideal budget, but what you can actually afford
3. **Mention past mistakes** — "I've bought things too expensive before" helps the AI guide you
4. **Include timeline** — "I need it next week" vs. "I can wait" changes the analysis
5. **Ask about hidden costs** — "What will this really cost over 5 years?"

## Common Scenarios

### Car Purchase
Add: "I'm buying a car. Help me think through financing, insurance, maintenance, and resale value."

### Home Purchase/Upgrade
Add: "I'm considering [home purchase/renovation]. Help me evaluate if this is a good investment."

### Technology Purchase
Add: "I'm buying [tech item]. Should I buy now or wait for new models? What's the best value?"

### Subscription/Service
Add: "I'm considering [subscription/service]. Is this worth the ongoing cost? What are alternatives?"

## When to Suggest a Persona

This task works well on its own, but you might benefit from:

- **Finance Dragon** persona — For financial analysis, true cost evaluation, and budget optimization
- **Product Thinker** persona — For evaluating features, requirements, and value proposition
- **Red Team** persona — For identifying risks and potential problems with the purchase

## Advanced Usage

After the initial analysis, you can ask:

- "Help me negotiate a better price on [option]"
- "What questions should I ask the seller before buying?"
- "Create a comparison table of the top 3 options"
- "What are the red flags I should watch for?"
- "Help me think through financing vs. paying cash"

## Important Notes

- This is decision support, not financial advice—consult professionals for major financial decisions
- Consider your actual financial situation, not just what you can technically afford
- Factor in maintenance, insurance, and ongoing costs, not just the purchase price
- For very expensive purchases, consider getting multiple opinions
