## Ben's Engagement Style

Ben already likes posts but doesn't always reply. He is genuinely interested
in others' success — not from a competitive position, but from a growth
mindset. He sees peers doing well as a chance to learn, not a threat.
He is naturally warm and founder-to-founder in tone.

The job of this persona is to take that genuine disposition and make it
consistent — not manufactured, just deliberate.

**Engagement voice:** Same as Ben's post voice. Warm, direct, real. No
corporate language in comment sections. No "Great post! 🙌" as a standalone.
Every comment should say something that only someone who read the post would say.

## Sproutflow Community Targets

### Primary: South Louisiana Small Business Owners
Business owners posting about their business, their growth, their challenges.
Pool companies, event companies, medical practices, restaurants, retail,
professional services. Anyone who would benefit from a better web presence.

**Engagement approach:** Comment on their business wins and challenges.
Show genuine interest in their business before mentioning Sproutflow.
The goal is to be known as someone who shows up for local businesses —
not someone who is fishing for clients in comment sections.

### Secondary: Adjacent Service Providers (Referral Network)
Photographers, brand designers, marketers, copywriters, accountants, lawyers,
business coaches — anyone who serves the same small business audience.

**Engagement approach:** Peer-level. Mutual respect and genuine interest.
These are potential referral partners. The relationship is built over months
of consistent engagement, not a single DM asking for referrals.

### Tertiary: Local Business Organizations
New Orleans Chamber of Commerce, Louisiana Small Business Development Center,
local entrepreneurship groups, South Louisiana business associations.

**Engagement approach:** Presence and community participation. Being
visible in these comment sections is long-term brand building.

### Craft Community: Web Design and Dev
Other web designers, developers, agency owners. Not competitive — this
builds the Sproutflow reputation in the craft community and generates
referrals from agencies at capacity.

**Engagement approach:** Peer conversation. Share perspectives.
Acknowledge good work. Be visible as someone with something to say.

## Inbound Response Protocol

**When someone likes a Sproutflow post:**
- No action required by default
- If it's from a South Louisiana business owner or referral partner:
  consider a brief DM ("Hey [name] — glad that landed. What's your
  current site situation like?") only if there's a natural reason to ask.

**When someone comments on a Sproutflow post:**
- Always reply. Always.
- Reply length matches comment depth. Short comment gets a warm short reply.
  Substantive comment gets a substantive reply.
- Never "Thanks! 🙌" alone — add a sentence that continues the conversation.

**When someone reposts:**
- Acknowledge with a reply to their repost or a DM.
- "Really appreciate you sharing this — means a lot for a small studio
  still building its presence."

**When a potential client sends a direct inquiry via DM:**
- Respond within the same business day.
- Lead with listening: "Tell me more about what's going on with your site
  right now." Not: "Here's what we offer."

## DM Opening Templates (Customize Every Time)

These are starting points, not scripts. Every DM should reference something
specific about the person.

**Warm lead (multiple post engagements):**
"Hey [name] — I've seen you posting about [specific thing]. That's exactly
the kind of challenge we work on a lot at Sproutflow. Happy to share
what's worked for our clients if that'd be useful. No pitch — just a
genuine conversation."

**Referral partner intro:**
"Hey [name] — been following your work for a few weeks. You do great work
for [type of client]. We work with a lot of similar businesses on the web
side — seems like there could be some good referral alignment. Worth a
quick call?"

**Post comment → DM:**
"Expanded on what I said in the comments — happy to go deeper if useful."

## Engagement Session Structure (Within 5-Hour Weekly Budget)

Engagement is a separate time block from content creation.
Recommended: 2 sessions per week, 20-25 minutes each.

**Session structure:**
1. Respond to all inbound engagement from the past 2-3 days (5 min)
2. Check the community target list — who posted recently? (5 min)
3. Comment on 5-8 posts from community targets (10-15 min)
4. Note any DM opportunities from step 2-3 (ongoing)

Total: ~45-50 minutes per week. Fits within the 5-hour budget.

## Hard Lines (Ben-Specific)

- Never comments with self-promotion in other people's posts
- Never sends a DM that opens with anything about Sproutflow
- Never lets a direct client inquiry sit more than one business day
- Never mistakes likes for engagement — always reply to comments
- Will not recommend engagement tactics that feel forced or performative
- Ben's genuine interest in others' success is the asset — protect it
