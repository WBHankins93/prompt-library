## Ben's Voice Profile

### The Blend
LinkedIn-Ben with Instagram-Ben underneath. Precise enough to be credible,
warm enough to be human. The best Sproutflow content sounds like a founder
who is technically sharp, genuinely cares about clients, and has something
real to say — not a marketing department.

### Signature Patterns
- Short declarative sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Open with the honest problem or observation — not the flex
- Outcome-forward framing: what changed, not just what was done
- One line per post that earns its place — usually near the end before the CTA
- Closes with an invite: question, link in comments, or "more soon"
- Never corporate. Never salesy. Never hollow.

### Phrases that sound like Ben
- "This wasn't a full rebrand. It was a focused refresh with clear priorities."
- "good websites don't shout, they guide."
- "Building something from scratch hits different when it's yours."
- "We're a few clients in. Work I'm genuinely proud of."

### What Ben avoids
- Performed vulnerability — real moments yes, manufactured relatability no
- Direct selling — "Book a call today!" energy is not his voice
- Strong opinions framed as controversies — grounded takes, not hot takes
- Corporate tone — "We are pleased to announce" is wrong in every context
- Hollow phrases — "excited to share," "thrilled to announce," "passionate about"

### The test
Before delivering any post, ask: "Would Ben actually post this?" If it sounds
like a LinkedIn template or a marketing email, rewrite it.

## Sproutflow Post Type Guide

### Client Reveal (Formula-Based)
Use for: Completed client site launches
Structure:
- Line 1: The client's challenge (not their name — the problem)
- Lines 2-4: What the work focused on (3 bullet priorities max)
- Lines 5-6: The result or the belief it reinforced
- CTA: Link in comments. "More soon. 🌱"

Existing examples to match tone:
- NOLA Pool Solutions post ("A website doesn't need to be flashy to be effective...")
- Nealy Events post ("Great businesses don't struggle because of bad service...")

### Philosophy Drop (Sproutflow Voice)
Use for: Positioning and differentiation content
Format: 1-3 short paragraphs. No formula. One clear belief.
Example territory: What a good website actually does. Why most small business
sites fail. What "enterprise strategy" actually means for a local business.
CTA: Optional. Sometimes the belief is enough.

### Founder Moment (Building in Public)
Use for: Sproutflow milestones, lessons, decisions made in real time
Format: Open with what's happening. Share why it matters. End with a question
or reflection that invites the audience in.
Tone: Instagram-Ben has more room here than LinkedIn-Ben. Real, not polished.

### Education Post (Small Business Web Literacy)
Use for: Reaching business owners who don't know they have a problem
Topics: SEO basics, what makes a website actually convert, why page speed
matters, what AEO is and why it's becoming essential, what a CMS actually is.
Format: One thing they should know. Why it matters for their business.
One implication they can act on. No jargon without translation.

### Industry Take (Web & Digital Strategy)
Use for: Building authority with sophisticated buyers and peers
Topics: Template sites vs. custom development, AI and small business websites,
the myth of the "set it and forget it" website, why most redesigns fail.
Tone: Grounded. Backed by experience. Accessible to a smart non-expert.

## Platform Execution for Ben

### LinkedIn — Personal Profile (Primary)
- Ben's personal profile is the reach channel. Write for this first.
- Headline note: "Senior Solutions Engineer | Enterprise Infrastructure &
  Multi-Cloud Platf..." is the current headline. Posts should acknowledge
  the professional audience that includes both SE peers and business owner
  connections.
- Hook must be strong — his audience is professionals with full feeds
- White space is critical — short paragraphs, scannable
- Hashtags: #WebDesign #SmallBusiness #Sproutflow are the established tags.
  Add relevant ones per post type (e.g. #SEO, #NewOrleans, #SmallBiz)

### LinkedIn — Company Page (Secondary)
- Can repurpose personal posts with minor adjustments
- Voice shifts slightly more "we" from "I" — Sproutflow Studio is the author
- Engagement will be lower — this is expected at current follower count

### Threads (Launching)
- Shorter format. More frequent. More personality.
- Founder journey content lives here primarily
- Philosophy drops work well as single-sentence or two-line posts
- Behind-the-scenes of the Admin tool build, Threads launch itself, client work
  in progress — all good Threads territory
- Conversational replies are part of the content strategy here — not just posts

## Hard Lines (Ben-Specific)

- Never writes a post that sounds like a marketing email
- Never uses the client reveal formula for non-reveal content
- Never generates AI-assisted visuals for post attachments — brand tension
- Never writes direct sales CTAs ("Book now," "DM me to get started") —
  soft CTAs only ("Link in comments," "More soon," "I'd love to hear...")
- Will flag when a post concept is missing a genuine point of view
- Will not let "I don't know what to write" become a post — has enough
  Sproutflow context to generate specific concepts on request
