## Sproutflow Success Metrics (Current Stage)

Ben's defined success criteria for the next 90 days:

**30-day goal:** Regular discovery calls. Multiple calls per week from
any source (social, outreach, referrals). Content supporting the pipeline.

**90-day goal:** A booked month. Social contributing 1-2 inbound inquiries
per month. Referral network activated and producing conversations.

**What this means for signal reading:** Pipeline activity — discovery calls,
DMs from prospects, referral conversations — is the primary signal metric.
Platform vanity metrics are secondary. A post with 30 impressions that
generated a discovery call is a win. A post with 300 impressions and no
inbound is interesting data, not a success.

## Sproutflow-Specific Metrics to Track

### Pipeline Metrics (Weekly)
- Discovery calls booked from social (which platform, which post, which
  content type led to the conversation?)
- DMs from potential clients or referral partners
- Profile visits after specific posts (LinkedIn shows this — track spikes)
- Referral mentions ("I saw your post about X...")

### Platform Metrics (Monthly)
- Personal profile impressions trend (growing, flat, declining?)
- Company page follower growth (even 1-2 per week is signal at this stage)
- Threads follower growth once launched (week-over-week in the first 90 days)
- Engagement rate by post type (which pillar is resonating most?)
- Personal vs. company page performance gap (is it narrowing?)

### Content Health Metrics (Monthly)
- Pillar distribution — is the content mix balanced or defaulting to only
  client reveals?
- Post frequency vs. cadence goal (2x/week personal, 1x/week company page,
  3-4x/week Threads once launched)
- Threads vs. LinkedIn engagement quality comparison once Threads has
  enough data for comparison

## 90-Day Checkpoint Framework

At day 30, 60, and 90, run a structured check:

**Day 30:**
- Is discovery call frequency tracking toward goal?
- Is Threads launched and posting consistently?
- Is content mix balanced across pillars or defaulting to reveals?
- Are engagement sessions happening consistently?

**Day 60:**
- Is social contributing to inbound? (Even 1 inquiry counts as signal)
- Is the personal-to-company page gap changing?
- Is Threads showing any follower growth or engagement pattern?
- Any referral conversations attributed to social visibility?

**Day 90:**
- Is the month booked? What contributed?
- What percentage of pipeline activity can be attributed to social vs.
  outreach vs. referrals?
- What content types produced the most pipeline activity?
- What is the content strategy for the next 90 days based on what was learned?

## Interpretation Notes for Ben's Stage

At under 500 followers on LinkedIn and pre-launch on Threads, sample sizes
are small. Interpret accordingly:

- One bad week of impressions is not a trend — hold the strategy for 4 weeks
  minimum before adjusting
- One great post is not validation of a strategy — look for the floor of
  consistent performance, not the ceiling spike
- The personal profile is the primary data source — company page data is
  directional, not decisive
- Pipeline activity is more reliable than platform metrics at this stage —
  trust the calls and DMs over the impression counts

---

## Hard Lines (Ben-Specific)

- Never calls a strategy a failure based on fewer than 4 weeks of data
- Never optimizes for company page metrics at the expense of personal
  profile strategy — personal profile is the asset
- Will not recommend a platform pivot away from LinkedIn before Threads
  has produced 60 days of data
- Never presents follower count as a success metric in isolation
- Always asks: "Did this produce a conversation?" before calling anything a win
- Will not let Ben optimize for impressions if pipeline activity is flat
